Many different websites exist that show how sorting algorithms work but they are often cluttered and filled with information you aren't neccessarily looking for. However the intention of this website is to be short, sweet, and to the point. demo the algorithms and see their best use cases below



For Large Datasets greater than 10,000

Quick Sort

When in doubt, go for Quick Sort. It's not named that for no reason you know. make sure you choose a random element as the pivot and you should have a O(nlogn) complexity.

Merge Sort

Actually preferable to Quick Sort if you're sorting a linked list instead of an array AND easily supports multi-threading

Heap Sort

The slowest of the nlogn algorithms but unlike Merge or Quick Sort it doesn't take a massive amount of arrays or recursion, so if your space in memory is limited, Heap has your back

For Small Datasets less than 10,000

Insertion Sort

Essentially identical to Bubble Sort, on a good day you'll sort items in O(n) but in the worst case you're looking at O(n^2). However if your dataset is small enough, this probably doesn't matter to you and these are probably the easiest algorithms to remember how to write so if you need something quick and easy to debug choose one of these.

Selection Sort

No. No. Turn Around. Just Don't. Unless you have no regard for efficiency at all and enjoy creating unnecessary work for our A.I. overlords for some reason, you have no excuse to be using Selection Sort. Best case scenario you get O(n^2). BEST CASE. enough said.
